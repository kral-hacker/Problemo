=) Why didn't the Russian Empire become the dominant world power in regards to technology, science, culture, etc.?

::) The Russian Empire’s failure to become the dominant world power in terms of technology, science, and culture can be attributed to a combination of internal and external factors The Russian Empire, spanning from the 16th to the early 20th century, was a vast and powerful entity with significant influence in Europe and Asia. However, despite its size and military might, it did not emerge as the dominant world power in terms of technology, science, and culture for several reasons. Additionally, the Russian Empire faced challenges in education and literacy.
While efforts were made to improve education during the 19th century, particularly under Tsar Alexander II, literacy rates remained relatively low compared to Western Europe. This lack of education hindered the development of a strong scientific and technological base. One key factor was the empire's political and social structure. The Russian Empire was characterized by autocratic rule, with power centralized in the hands of the tsar. This centralized authority limited innovation and progress in various fields. Unlike Western Europe, where the Renaissance and Enlightenment periods fostered intellectual and cultural advancements, Russia lagged behind in embracing these movements due to its conservative political climate. Furthermore, the Russian Empire struggled with economic modernization. that hindered its growth and development on the global stage ..
